# Research Brief: Premium Pricing & Affiliate Commission Rates

## What I need
Research and recommend the optimal premium subscription price and photographer affiliate commission structure for Salt Safari.

## About Salt Safari
A website (launching as a PWA) where everyday swimmers, snorkellers and divers discover what marine species appear at each dive/snorkel location in Australia — without needing to know what iNaturalist is.

**Target audience:** Casual snorkellers and swimmers, not just certified divers. Think families, tourists, weekend beachgoers who want to know "what can I see here?" Australian market first (Sydney, Central Coast), expanding nationally then internationally.

**Premium tier is called "Know the Ocean"** and includes:
1. **"In Season Now" alerts** — email notifications telling you which species are at your favourite spots right now, based on iNaturalist seasonality data + local knowledge. E.g. "Port Jackson sharks have been commonly spotted at Bare Island this month."
2. **Species deep dives** — extended stories behind each species: behaviour, fun facts, ID tips, cultural significance, conservation status. Makes you the interesting person in the group.

**Free tier includes:** Browse locations, species summaries, species ID tool (Merlin Bird ID-style stepped filter). Free content is fully indexable for SEO.

## Pricing research needed

### Questions to answer
1. **What do comparable niche content/nature apps charge?** Look at:
   - Merlin Bird ID (free, but funded by Cornell — what would it cost if it weren't?)
   - eBird (free but donation-funded)
   - iNaturalist (free)
   - Seek by iNaturalist (free)
   - FishBrain (fishing app with premium — very relevant, similar model)
   - AllTrails (hiking, freemium — good pricing comp)
   - PeakFinder, PeakVisor (mountain ID apps)
   - Snorkel/dive-specific apps if any exist with premium tiers
   - Any marine life ID apps with subscriptions
2. **What's the willingness to pay for niche outdoor/nature content?** Consider that the audience is casual (not hardcore divers who already spend $$$). Many will be occasional snorkellers.
3. **Monthly vs. annual pricing** — what's standard? What discount for annual? (e.g. $7/month or $50/year)
4. **Should there be a free trial?** If so, how long? What converts best for content subscriptions?
5. **Australian market considerations** — AUD pricing, local spending habits for apps/subscriptions
6. **Price anchoring** — at what price does this feel like an impulse buy vs. something that needs justification?

### Constraints
- Price needs to be low enough that a photographer saying "subscribe to know what's in the water" feels like an easy ask to their audience
- Price needs to be high enough that 30% affiliate commission is worth a photographer's time to promote
- We're a new, unknown brand — can't charge like an established platform
- The value is ongoing (seasonal alerts change monthly) not one-time

## Affiliate commission research needed

### Questions to answer
1. **What commission rates do comparable affiliate programs offer?** Look at:
   - AllTrails affiliate/referral program
   - FishBrain
   - Strava
   - Content subscription platforms (Substack, Patreon creator referrals)
   - SaaS affiliate programs in the $5-15/month range
   - Outdoor/adventure brand affiliate programs
2. **Recurring vs. one-time commission** — what's more attractive to creators? What's standard?
   - If recurring: for how long? (3 months, 6 months, 12 months, lifetime?)
   - If one-time: what flat rate or percentage?
3. **What makes a photographer actually promote something?** The commission needs to be worth their time given their typical audience size (1K-100K followers). Model out: if a photographer has 10K Instagram followers and 2% convert, what do they earn at various commission rates?
4. **Cookie duration** — how long should the attribution window be? (7 days, 30 days, 90 days?)
5. **Two-tier consideration** — should early photographers get better rates to incentivise being first?

### Deliverable
Recommend a specific pricing structure (monthly + annual) and a specific affiliate commission structure with reasoning. Include a simple model showing photographer earnings at different audience sizes.
