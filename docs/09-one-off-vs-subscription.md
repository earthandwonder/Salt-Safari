## Converting users without an App Store buy button

### Give three free IDs before asking for payment

RevenueCat's landmark 2025 A/B test of ~12,500 users found that web-only purchase flows converted at **18.1% versus 27.0% for native App Store IAP** â€” a roughly one-third reduction. Only **44% of users who tapped "Buy" on a web checkout completed payment**, compared to 71% for App Store one-tap purchase. This is the single biggest conversion risk for a PWA.

The recommended free trial structure: **"Your first 3 species IDs are free."** This lets users experience the wizard's core value before encountering payment friction. After three IDs, the app prompts purchase with a clear value anchor. Three is enough to demonstrate utility without giving away the product â€” it covers roughly one snorkelling session. Species deep-dive content should show the first paragraph free with a "Read the full story" prompt behind purchase.

**Mitigating PWA payment friction requires three specific technical choices:**

First, implement **Apple Pay and Google Pay as the primary payment buttons**. These replicate the one-tap App Store experience on web and are the single most effective friction reducer. Second, use **Stripe's embedded checkout** rather than redirecting to a separate payment page â€” every redirect loses ~15% of users. Third, keep the purchase form minimal: just email + payment method. No account creation required before purchase.

The PWA model does carry one significant advantage: **zero App Store commission**. Apple takes 15â€“30% of every IAP; Stripe takes only ~2.9% + A$0.30. On a A$9.99 sale, Salt Safari keeps **A$9.40** (PWA/Stripe) versus **A$7.00â€“$8.50** (App Store). This A$1.50â€“$2.40 per-sale advantage funds the generous 40â€“50% affiliate commission.
