# Scuba diving & snorkeling at Bare Island

Bare Island is among the best and most popular scuba diving sites in Sydney. Accessible from the mainland by a footbridge, it has many fantastic areas to explore and species to discover, just expect to share the site (especially on weekends).

There are two main dives here which you can do in the day or night; The **east side** and the **west side**. Both are accessible in most weather and generally have good visibility.

## 🤿 Keep reading

The east side offers better visibility and is shallower, so it tends to be better for snorkelers and beginner divers. Along its long rocky reef you may see weedy sea dragons, abundant populations of nudibranchs on rock formations, and the pygmy pipehorse.

The west side dive is the better option if there’s a southerly swell. You may see rocky reef walls, sponge gardens, Port Jackson sharks, red Indian fish and gurnards.

On both sides you may see horn sharks, pygmy pipefish, weedy seadragons, wobbegong sharks, as well as sponges, corals, and vibrant nudibranches living between the rocks. Make sure to bring a torch to fully appreciate their rich colours.
