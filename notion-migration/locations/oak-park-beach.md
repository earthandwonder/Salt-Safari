# Scuba diving & snorkeling at Oak Park

Oak Park Rock Pool is a historic Sydney pool, having been built in 1931. But for scuba diving and snorkeling, we’ll be heading out the back and into the reef beyond. Enter on either the north or south side of the pool depending on conditions. Swim over to the south-eastern corner of the tide pool, then head East for about 30 meters.

You’ll encounter an underwater wall that you’ll follow for most of the dive. Swim over it so it’s on your left, and inspect the rocky overhangs and cracks for critters.

## 🤿 Keep reading…

Check your surroundings as you might be accompanied by schools of fish or a curious Blue Groper. Then follow the wall east until it separates into a bit of a rocky pile. The wall will gradually take a turn to the North, which you can follow to find a cave that three divers could fit inside.

From here if you have enough air you can head North-East to find ‘fish soup’, a drop-off that’s often full of large schools of fish.

Oak Park is great for all levels of scuba divers, and can be done as a night dive. Look out for Port Jackson sharks, blue gropers, cuttlefish, crayfish, seahorses, sea turtles, and lots of weird coral life.

## 🐠 Look out for these species

- Blue Groper
- Port Jackson Shark
- Stingray
- Nudibranch
- Sea Turtle
- Cuttlefish
- Pigmy Pipehorse
